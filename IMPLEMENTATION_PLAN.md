    - **Window size: 36 months (not 24).** Worked through the arithmetic explicitly rather than taking the 24-month suggestion at face value: `detectCadence`'s `.yearly` window accepts gaps of **362–369 days** between consecutive occurrences, and detection requires `occurrenceCount >= 3` (two gaps). A yearly-cadence cluster's oldest-to-newest span can therefore legitimately be up to `2 × 369 = 738` days ≈ 24.3 months — a 24-month (≈730-day) window would clip the oldest of 3 yearly occurrences right at the boundary in the worst case, silently dropping a real yearly subscription down to 2 occurrences (below the `>= 3` floor) and defeating detection entirely — the opposite of R10's goal. 36 months (≈1095 days) leaves roughly 357 days (~1 year) of slack beyond the worst-case yearly span, comfortably covering calendar-month/leap-year arithmetic edge cases in whatever windowing helper T16b adds (e.g. `calendar.date(byAdding: .month, value: -36, to: now)`), while still meaningfully bounding how far back a single anomalous historical charge (like a stale trial price) can reach to poison the `allSatisfy` stability gate for monthly/weekly/biweekly clusters, where 36 months is already generously more history than needed to establish a stable mode amount.
